Old Way vs. New Way
Who maintains the knowledge?
Traditional PKM
01
You write every note
Read source, decide what matters, find the words — all manually, every time.
02
You tag, link & maintain
Build the graph by hand. The system slowly drifts unless you keep tending it.
03
You search & hope
Ctrl+F and pray you tagged it right. Often ends in "I know I wrote this somewhere."
LLM-Compiled Wiki
01
You collect raw material
Articles, papers, repos, screenshots → drop into raw/. No formatting needed.
02
LLM compiles the wiki
Summaries, concept nodes, backlinks, index files — the model structures everything. You judge, not write.
03
Outputs write back in
Q&A answers, slides, charts → saved as files → fed back into the wiki. It compounds automatically.

The Core Workflow
The Compile Loop
How an LLM-maintained knowledge base runs — and compounds over time.
📥
Collect, don't curate
Drop raw material in first. Let the model structure it. Separate raw from compiled.
🔍
Clear structure beats RAG
At ~100 articles, good Markdown indexes let the agent navigate without vector search.
♻️
Outputs compound
Answers and slides are files. Write them back in. The wiki grows smarter with each loop.
👁️
You read, LLM maintains
Knowledge Night is your browser. The model is the maintainer. Human role: judge and direct.
